Discover the Cities of Morocco

Marrakech | Casablanca | Fes | Tangier

Marrakech

Marrakech, known as the “Red City” due to its distinctive red sandstone buildings, is a vibrant and bustling city in central Morocco. It is famous for its historic medina, a UNESCO World Heritage site, which is home to the lively Jemaa el-Fnaa square, traditional souks, and stunning palaces like the Bahia Palace. The city is also known for its beautiful gardens, such as the Majorelle Garden, and the iconic Koutoubia Mosque. Marrakech offers a unique blend of traditional Moroccan culture and modern amenities, with luxury hotels, spas, and golf courses. The city’s rich history, vibrant arts scene, and dynamic nightlife make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Moroccan experience.

Casablanca

Casablanca, often referred to as “Casa,” is Morocco’s largest city and a key economic hub. Situated on the Atlantic coast, it boasts a blend of modern architecture and traditional Moroccan culture. The city is famous for the Hassan II Mosque, one of the largest mosques in the world, which stands majestically by the sea. Casablanca’s port is one of the busiest in Africa, contributing significantly to the country’s economy. The city also features a vibrant nightlife, with the Corniche area offering numerous restaurants, cafes, and nightclubs. The architecture in Casablanca is a unique mix of Moorish and Art Deco styles, reflecting its colonial past. Additionally, the city is home to several cultural institutions, including museums, theaters, and galleries. The historic medina provides a glimpse into the traditional Moroccan way of life, contrasting with the city’s modern business districts. Casablanca’s cosmopolitan atmosphere and rich history make it a fascinating destination for visitors.

Fes

Fes is the spiritual capital of Morocco, home to the oldest university in the world, the University of Al Quaraouiyine, and the medieval medina of Fes el Bali.

Tangier

Tangier, located at the northern tip of Morocco, is a city with a rich and diverse history. Overlooking the Strait of Gibraltar, it has been a strategic gateway between Africa and Europe for centuries. Tangier is known for its white-washed buildings, bustling medina, and the historic Kasbah. The city has a cosmopolitan atmosphere, influenced by various cultures, including Phoenician, Roman, and Arab. Key attractions include the Caves of Hercules, Cape Spartel, and the American Legation Museum. Tangier’s unique blend of history, culture, and stunning coastal views make it a fascinating destination for visitors.
